Floating point numbers >>>
what is float ?
Floating point numbers are imprecise. There are times when a number cannot be imagined at all. For example, the result of calculating 1.01 - 0.99 would be 0.020000000000000018 - a number very close to the expected number, but not the same.

Like integers, floating point numbers have a specific size (32 bits or 64 bits). Using a larger size increases precision (how many digits we can use to calculate)

In addition to numbers, there are several other values such as: (NaN, for things like 0/0) and positive and negative infinity (+∞ and −∞).
